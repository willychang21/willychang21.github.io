## **<u>Passage 11</u>　jj**

　Was Felix Mendelssohn (1809–1847) a great **composer(作曲家)**? On its face, the question seems **absurd(荒誕)**. One of the most gifted **prodigies(天才)** in the history of music, he produced his first masterpiece at sixteen. From then on, he was recognized as an artist of **preternatural(超自然)** abilities, <a class="text-danger">**not only**</a> as a composer <a class="text-danger">**but also**</a> as a pianist and **conductor(指揮家)**. <a class="text-danger">**But**</a> Mendelssohn's enduring popularity has often been **at odds(不一致)** — sometimes quite sharply — with his critical standing. <a class="text-danger">**Despite**</a> general acknowledgment of his genius, there has been a noticeable <mark>reluctance</mark> to rank him with, say, Schumann or Brahms. **As Haggin put it(正如Haggin所說)**, Mendelssohn, as a composer, was a “minor master . . . working on a small scale of emotion and texture.”

1. **Select a sentence in the passage whose function is to indicate the range of Mendelssohn’s musical talents.**

    Ans : Fourth sentence (“From then. . . conductor”)

2. **The passage suggests that anyone attempting to evaluate Mendelssohn’s career must confront which of the following dichotomies(二分法)**    
    - The **tension(張力)** between Mendelssohn’s career as a composer and his career as a pianist and conductor  
    - The contrast between Mendelssohn’s popularity and that of Schumann and Brahms  
    - The **discrepancy(差異)** between Mendelssohn’s popularity and his **standing(地位)** among critics  
    - The inconsistency between Mendelssohn’s reputation during his lifetime and his reputation since his death  
    - The gap between Mendelssohn’s prodigious musical beginnings and his decline in later years

    Ans : (C)
3. **It can be inferred that the “reluctance” mentioned in the passage is being ascribed(歸因於) to**
    - most composers since Mendelssohn
    - Schumann and Brahms
    - the music-listening public
    - music critics generally
    - Haggin exclusively

    Ans : ~~(B)~~(D)
    > (B)不可能啦它們是比較對象耶，選(D)的原因是後面 "As Haffin put it..." 這表示 Haffin 也是其中一個不情願的 music critics

4. **The author mentions Schumann and Brahms primarily(主要) in order to(為了)**
    - provide examples of composers who are often compared with Mendelssohn
    - identify certain composers who are more popular than Mendelssohn
    - identify composers whom Mendelssohn influenced
    - establish the **milieu(環境)** in which Mendelssohn worked
    - establish a standard of comparison for Mendelssohn as a composer

    Ans : (E)

## **<u>Passage 12</u>　jj**

　Scientific consensus is that humans first began to have a warming effect on Earth’s climate within the past century, after coal-burning factories, power plants, and motor vehicles began releasing carbon dioxide (CO2) and other **greenhouse(溫室)** gases in significant quantities into the air.  
　<mark><a class="text-danger"><strong>However</strong></a>, evidence suggests that human agricultural activities may have had such an effect much earlier: <strong>concentrations(濃度)</strong> of CO2 started rising about 8000 years ago, <a class="text-danger" ><strong>even though</strong></a> natural trends indicate they should have been dropping; <strong>methane(甲烷)</strong> levels rose similarly about 3,000 years later.</mark> Without these rises, <a class="text-danger">**however**</a>, current temperatures in northern parts of North America and Europe would be cooler by three to four degrees Celsius—enough to **inhibit(抑制)** agriculture—and an ice age would probably have begun several thousand years ago in northeastern Canada.
